However, lets back this visual analysis with some regression analysis.

assumptions:

  1. variables are independent.

  2. observations between quarters are independent.

  3. the time series covered by the data is indicative of a broader time scale.

  4. independent and identically distributed (iid) normal variables with mean zero and variance.

Generally speaking there is a doubt about the autocorrelation of errors in time series data. In this case however, the dates are somewhat apart and We can assume that it is not a major issue.

Multiple R-squared: 0.01137

1 % of the variability in the live register can be explained by the regression on the planning permissions data. This is very low and suggests no relationship.

F-statistic is 0.1609/t- value is 0.401 .

P value ( 0.6943) is greater than 5% so accept null hypothesis. There is no relationship between the planning permissions and the live register.

Planning permissions data should not be used to predict live register data.

What can we take from this ?

Just because planning permission has been given in a certain quarter doesnt mean that the work will start in that quarter or at all.

Construction workers signing off the live register in Cork are doing so for construction jobs in more than just Cork city.

The planning data is confined to residential planning, so perhaps if Commercial planning permissions were included, it may change the output.
